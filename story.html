Our Story

Born from the
open road.

The name came from a Rivian paint color. The business came from the belief that coffee should be straightforward, traceable, and worth the drive.

It started with a truck and a question: what if roasting could happen at the point of gathering, powered by the vehicle that brought us there? The original setup was a Rivian R1T in Red Canyon paint, and that color became our name.

The truck-bed concept created momentum, but quality required control. Roasting moved indoors so profile consistency could improve without losing the open-road identity that made it matter.

That shift created the operating model we still use: strict batch sizing, single-origin focus, and direct community testing at events where people tell you exactly what works.

What we roast.

Red Canyon sources from three anchor regions: Ethiopian Yirgacheffe, Kenyan Nyeri AA, and Kona Extra Fancy from Hawai'i. We roast for clarity and sweetness first, never for generic dark-roast uniformity.

Each lot is cupped repeatedly and adjusted for brew versatility, so the same bag performs in pour-over, drip, and travel setups without taste collapse.

Where you'll find us.

Red Canyon is the exclusive coffee roaster for all Rangeway Basecamps. While EVs charge, guests get freshly brewed single-origin coffee with the option to take bags to go.

Outside Basecamps, we serve Bay Area Rivian Club and NorCal EV events, where product decisions are tested in real environments, not only in controlled tasting rooms.

Why it matters.

The coffee market has enough noise already. Red Canyon focuses on clear provenance, clear flavor, and clear service standards. If a coffee does not stand out in the cup, it does not stay in rotation.

We built this brand for people who value the route as much as the destination: disciplined, practical, and still open to detours.
